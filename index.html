Prevention from Identity Theft

Identity theft is a serious issue that involves stealing an individual's personal or financial data to use their identity for fraudulent activities. It can occur in various forms, including financial identity theft, tax identity theft, criminal identity theft, and medical identity theft.

How Does Identity Theft Occur?

Cybercriminals use several techniques to steal your identity:

  • Phishing: This involves sending emails or messages that appear to be from legitimate sources, such as banks, which contain links to fake websites that require users to enter sensitive information.
  • Data breaches: This involves hacking into banks’ and other organizations’ databases to acquire customer information, which can then be used for a variety of fraudulent activities.
  • Social engineering: This involves manipulating individuals to reveal confidential information, such as by impersonating bank representatives and requesting personal details through telephone calls or social media.
  • Card skimming: This involves the use of skimming devices attached to ATMs and card readers, which can capture card information during legitimate transactions.
  • Dumpster diving: This involves searching through discarded documents such as bank statements and credit card statements.

How Can You Protect Yourself?

Here are some tips to protect yourself from identity theft:

  • Monitor Your Bank Accounts: Regularly review your bank and credit card records for any suspicious transactions.
  • Use Strong Passwords: Do not share your PIN with anyone on or off the phone. Use a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols in your passwords.
  • Two-factor Authentication: Use two-factor notification for emails. This adds an extra layer of security by requiring you to enter a unique code sent to your phone or email when logging in.
  • Secure Your Devices: Secure all your devices with a password. Install reliable security software and keep it updated. Encrypt your data where possible.
  • Be Cautious with Software: Don’t install random software from the internet. Always download software from trusted sources.
  • Be Careful on Social Media: Don’t post sensitive information over social media. Cybercriminals can use the information you share on social media to answer 'challenge' questions on your accounts and gain access to your money and personal information.
  • Avoid Unknown Calls and Messages: Do not answer phone calls, texts, social media messages, or email from numbers or people you do not know. Be wary of free trial offers, and always research the company first.
  • Don't Share Personal Information: Do not share personal information like your bank account number, Social Security number, or date of birth unless you initiated the contact and know who you're dealing with.

Remember, prevention is always better than cure. Stay safe!
